About Me

Who I am beyond the work

What I care about

I care about the world in which I live — not in the abstract, but specifically.

I care about where this country is headed. I care about fairness and justice, about the fact that people are hurting and that the systems meant to protect them have been bent out of shape. I've watched justice become something that works differently depending on who you are and how much you have, and I find that intolerable in both directions. The rich shouldn't be above it and the poor shouldn't be failed by it. I care not just about doing the right thing but about doing it the right way.

I try to make things better when I can. I try to tell the truth even when it costs me something. I try to be a force for good and to fight the part of me that whispers that none of it matters. That voice is a liar, and I'm working on it.

What I believe

I believe I could be more than I am.

I believe that the primary obstacle between me and that version of myself is me — and I take that seriously enough to do something about it. I believe I have something important to say, and I believe I've spent too long convincing myself otherwise. Both of those things are true at once, and sitting with that tension is part of the work.

I move through the world oriented toward action and solution. I want to name problems plainly, find people smarter than me and learn from them, and put in the work to make things better rather than just talk about them. I'm drawn to the problems nobody has figured out yet — justice, homelessness, the things that could be fixed but aren't because of politics or inertia or a general misreading of the situation.

Art, community, building

The through-line connecting everything I do.

Art is both a product and a process. The process is how I express what exists inside me. The product is the physical manifestation of that — something that can be held, seen, experienced by someone else. Whether I'm working in leather or wood or paint or building something digital, what I care about most is the idea and the design. The medium is just the language.

This is the same instinct that drives the Sovereign Grid Initiative and every other project I take seriously. I'm interested in learning things in order to build things that other people can use. I think about the end user first. I like good design. I like when something works the way it was meant to work, for the person it was meant to work for.

What I care about

Building
I'm not interested in executing someone else's vision indefinitely. I'm interested in building something that outlasts me — something that matters after I'm gone from it.
Honesty & Vulnerability
Honesty is telling the truth. Vulnerability is telling you the ugly truth before you asked. I'm working to be both, as consistently as I can, in every room I'm in.
Wisdom & Knowledge
I want to learn how to think better. I want to ask better questions. I want to understand more — about the world, about people, about why things are the way they are.

Outside the work

How I spend my time.

I read nonfiction — books on history, politics, religion, and culture, and books that help me understand myself better. Books that make me think about why I do what I do and how I might do it differently.

I write. I paint. I work in leather and wood. I bind books. I'm interested in metalwork and forging and a long list of crafts I haven't gotten to yet. I care more about the ideas behind the things I make than the medium I make them in. The craft is in service of the concept, not the other way around.
